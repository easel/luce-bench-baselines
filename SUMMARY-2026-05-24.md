Hardest-of-the-hard subset (mix of cross-backend disagreements and

| Case                                        | 4k    | 8k   | 16k   | 32k   | 65k   |

| `aime2025-18` (ans=82)                      | 2     | 2    | 4     | 1     | 12    |

**Findings:**
1. **Two cases flip with budget** — `aime2025-01` only passes at 4k
   (overthinks at higher); `aime2025-02` only passes at 65k (needs
   full reasoning room).
2. **Per-case optimal budgets exist** — no single global value would
   catch both. Validates the "per-case budget" hypothesis.
3. **5 of 8 cases are budget-insensitive** — `recNu`, `rec4Uq`,
   `recDyt` give the *same* wrong answer at every budget (likely
   model-knowledge failure, not reasoning). `6082513...` terminates
   in 29s at every budget with identical wrong answer (natural EOS,
   confident-wrong). `aime2025-16` and `aime2025-18` bounce between
   different wrong numerics (sampling noise, never lands on truth).

## Bragi no-think run — same 8 cases, `--no-think`

**Complete: 5/8 PASS = 62.5%.** Versus best-of-any-thinking-budget = 2/8 = 25%.

| Case                    | No-think result | Wall  | Best with thinking |
|-------------------------|-----------------|-------|--------------------|
| `recNu` (GPQA, ans=B)   | **PASS ✓** `B`  | 80s   | FAIL all 5 budgets |
| `aime2025-01` (ans=70)  | **PASS ✓** `70` | 22s   | PASS at 4k only    |
| `aime2025-16` (ans=468) | **PASS ✓** `468`| 20s   | FAIL all 5 budgets |
| `rec4Uq` (GPQA, ans=B)  | **PASS ✓** `B`  | 24s   | FAIL all 5 budgets |
| `aime2025-02` (ans=588) | FAIL `0`        | 192s  | **PASS at 65k only** |
| `6082` (SGPQA, ans=A)   | FAIL `E`        | 14s   | FAIL all 5 budgets (knowledge) |
| `recDyt` (GPQA, ans=A)  | **PASS ✓** `A`  | 160s  | FAIL all 5 budgets |
| `aime2025-18` (ans=82)  | FAIL `48`       | 153s  | FAIL all 5 budgets |

**Headline:** no-think recovers 4 cases (recNu, aime2025-16, rec4Uq, recDyt — all GPQA
Diamond) that failed at *every* thinking budget. It loses on `aime2025-02` which
genuinely needs 65k of thinking. Cleanly proves no global mode wins.

### Per-case picker upper bound

If we could route each case to its optimal mode (no-think vs 4k/65k thinking),
this 8-case subset would hit **6/8 = 75%**:

| Mode                          | PASS on this subset |
|-------------------------------|---------------------|
| Always think, best single budget | 2/8 (25%)        |
| Always **no-think**           | 5/8 (62.5%)         |
| **Per-case picker**           | **6/8 (75%)**       |

The decision rule looks suite-aware:
- **GPQA Diamond** → default no-think (3/3 wins here)
- **AIME2025** → split: small problems → no-think or 4k; hard ones → 65k
- **SuperGPQA** → no signal on this case (knowledge failure)

## What the data argues for

1. **No-think is a stronger default than any thinking budget** on
   GPQA Diamond — 3/3 GPQA cases here flipped FAIL → PASS by removing
   the thinking phase. The 4 cases that thinking *can't* solve at any
   of 4k/8k/16k/32k/65k all pass without it.
2. **Per-case mode picker** would beat both no-think and any single
   budget — 6/8 = 75% achievable here vs 5/8 (no-think) or 2/8 (best
   single thinking budget). The picker dimensions are suite + (maybe)
   difficulty signal.
3. **Long thinking still wins specific cases** — `aime2025-02` only
   passes at 65k thinking, and fails with no-think (`0`). The hard-AIME
   tier genuinely needs the headroom.
4. **Sampling-mode retries are useful** for cloud-style servers
   (vidar) where re-running can flip a result. Greedy (cpp-server)
   doesn't get this benefit but is reproducible.
